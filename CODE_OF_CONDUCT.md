# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

AgriSnapp is a student-led, open-source project committed to building food security tools for farming communities worldwide. We are committed to making participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone.

## Our Standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the farmers we serve
- Showing empathy towards other community members

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- The use of sexualised language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email address) without explicit permission
- Any other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including the GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, code review comments), and any other channels where community members represent the project. It also applies to interactions in public spaces when an individual is representing AgriSnapp.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported by contacting the project maintainers at:

**raible.rebecca@gmail.com**

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The maintainer team is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident.

### Enforcement Process

1. **Report** — Submit a report via email or using GitHub's built-in [reporting tools](https://docs.github.com/en/communities/maintaining-your-safety-on-github/reporting-abuse-or-spam).
2. **Acknowledgement** — The maintainer team will acknowledge receipt of the report within 48 hours.
3. **Review** — The team will review the incident, gather context, and determine an appropriate response. The reporter will be kept informed throughout.
4. **Action** — Depending on the severity and nature of the violation, the maintainer team may take one or more of the following actions:

| Severity | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Minor / first offence | Private written warning with clarification of expected behaviour |
| Repeated or more serious | Temporary suspension from participation in project spaces |
| Severe or sustained | Permanent ban from all project spaces |

5. **Appeal** — Any party subject to enforcement action may appeal by responding to the maintainer's email within 14 days. Appeals will be reviewed by a maintainer not involved in the original decision.

### Confidentiality

All reports are treated as confidential. Identities of reporters will not be disclosed without explicit consent. The maintainer team will not retaliate against anyone who makes a good-faith report.
